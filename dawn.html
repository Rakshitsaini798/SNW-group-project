Orbiting Dawn

A quiet engineer is paired with a fallen prince on a silent-run cargo ship. Slow-burn banter, found family, and an AI that ships them harder than the crew does.

Enemies-to-lovers Sci-fi soft T4T leads

Story

On deck twelve, the AI dimmed the stars to help the prince sleep. The engineer stayed up anyway, humming a half-remembered flight song the ship seemed to enjoy. They traced lazy constellations along the viewport—routes no royal court would ever sign off on.

“You’re awake,” the prince murmured, voice barely louder than the reactor’s low hum.

“Systems don’t really sleep,” the engineer said. “And people planning to steal a kingdom back? They barely nap.”

The prince laughed—soft, shaky, like relief pretending to be confidence. “You think we can actually pull it off?”

“We’re already doing it,” the engineer said, nodding toward the cargo bay. The crew was running silent drills again, moving like shadows. The AI washed the walls in a slow lavender glow—its version of a thumbs-up. Somewhere below, someone fiddled with a battered radio, trying to drown nerves with old love songs.

Later, when an alarm hiccuped and faded, the engineer covered the prince’s trembling hands. “We’ll rewrite the flight plan as many times as we need,” they said. “Dawn’s not a destination—it’s a choice we keep making together.”
